Final Project: Crime Patterns in the Capital: Exploring Crime Patterns Across Washington D.C. in 2025

This analysis examines crime patterns in Washington, D.C. using 2025 incident data from the DC Open Data Portal. The project explores spatial, temporal, and categorical dimensions of crime, including offense distribution, ward-level disparities, reporting delays, and seasonal trends. Using Python-based data cleaning, geospatial processing, and visualization tools, the study identifies key patterns such as the dominance of property crimes, uneven crime distribution across wards, and clustering of incidents in specific neighborhoods. Overall, this project demonstrates how structured data analysis can reveal meaningful insights into urban crime dynamics and support evidence-based understanding of public safety trends in Washington, D.C.
